Signal Framework

How culture signals are measured and interpreted

Weak Signals (Growth)

x1
Missed Deadline
If a task passes its due date, 1 weak signal is automatically recorded. This surfaces where support or capacity adjustments may be needed.
x1
Needs Support
If a task is completed but the result needs improvement, 1 weak signal is recorded. This is a coaching cue, not a judgment.
x2
Blunder
When a task is completed but with notable mistakes or poor quality, 2 weak signals are recorded. This is a stronger coaching cue indicating repeated or avoidable errors.
x3
Critical Incident
For serious process failures, 3 weak signals are recorded. This indicates a systemic issue requiring attention, not individual blame.

Strong Signals (Strength)

x1
Completed with Excellence
When a task is completed and meets all expectations, 1 strong signal is recorded. Recognizes consistent, reliable delivery.
x2
Exceptional Contribution
When work exceeds expectations and creates outsized impact, 2 strong signals are recorded. Celebrates innovation and initiative.

Overview — What is GOR Framework?

Understanding the Culture Intelligence Console

The GOR Framework (Green-Orange-Red) is a Culture Intelligence Console designed for leadership teams. It replaces traditional performance management with a signal-based culture health tracking system.

Core Philosophy: This system measures culture health direction, not individual performance scores. Signals are coaching cues, not judgments. The goal is to create psychological safety while maintaining accountability.

Key Principles

  • Signals, Not Judgments — "Weak signal" instead of "red flag." Language shapes perception.
  • Culture Momentum — We measure direction (trends), not just position (snapshots).
  • Psychological Safety — Calm dashboards produce better leadership decisions.
  • Leadership Maturity — Clean signals for deliberate, accountable decisions.

What The System Tracks

  • Team Members — Your team roster with roles and status
  • Tasks — Work assignments with deadlines and quality review
  • Signals — Strong and Weak culture indicators
  • Zones — Strong / Steady / Weak health classification per member
  • Reports — Executive-level analytics and culture intelligence

Team Members

Adding, managing, and tracking team members

Adding a Member

  1. Go to Members from the sidebar
  2. Click + Add Member
  3. Fill in: Full Name, Email, Role (select from defined roles), and Status
  4. Click Save Member

Member Card

Each member card displays:

  • Zone Badge — Green, Orange, or Red based on net score
  • Net Score — Effective strong signals minus effective weak signals
  • Role — The assigned department/role
  • Trend Indicator — Accelerating, Steady, or Needs Attention

Member Actions

Star Exceptional Contribution — Record +2 strong signals for outstanding work
Crown Leadership Signal — Recognize mentoring, collaboration, or peer references (+1 each)
Alert Critical Incident — Log a serious process failure (+3 weak signals)
History Signal History — View all signals with dates, reasons, categories, and notes

Editing & Deleting

Click the pencil icon to edit a member's details, or the trash icon to delete (with confirmation).

Tasks

Creating, assigning, and managing work

Creating a Task

  1. Go to Tasks from the sidebar
  2. Click + Add Task
  3. Fill in: Title, Description (optional), Assignee, Priority, Due Date, Notes
  4. Click Save Task
Risk Alert: When assigning a task, the system checks if the member has 2+ weak signals in the same category or with matching keywords this month. A warning will appear but you can still assign the task.

Task Statuses

Pending — Not yet started
In Progress — Currently being worked on
Hold — Paused or blocked
Completed — Done (triggers review)
Cancelled — No longer needed
Overdue — Past due date (auto-detected)

Filtering Tasks

Use the filter bar at the top to show tasks by status: All, Pending, In Progress, Hold, Completed, Cancelled, or Overdue. You can also filter by time period using the calendar icon.

Task Table Columns

The task table shows: Task Title, Assignee, Priority, Due Date, Status, Category Badge (if signals were recorded), and Action buttons (edit, review, delete).

Automatic Keywords

Keywords are automatically extracted from the task title and description. These are used by the Risk Alert system to warn about repeated patterns when assigning tasks.

05

Signal System

Understanding green and weak signals

Signals are the core unit of measurement in the GOR Framework. They come in two types:

Strong Signals

Positive indicators of culture contribution and quality work.

  • Exceptional Contribution — +2 signals (exceeds expectations)
  • Completed with Excellence — +1 signal (meets all requirements)
  • Leadership Signal — +1 per criterion (mentoring, collaboration, peer reference)

Weak Signals

Coaching cues indicating areas for support and development.

  • Missed Deadline — +1 signal (automatic)
  • Needs Support — +1 signal (work needs improvement)
  • Blunder — +2 signals (notable mistakes)
  • Critical Incident — +3 signals (serious process failure)

How Signals Are Created

  • Automatically — Missed deadlines generate weak signals when a task passes its due date
  • Via Task Review — When marking a task as completed, the reviewer selects a quality assessment
  • Manually — Extraordinary contributions, leadership signals, and critical incidents can be logged from member cards

Signal Properties

Each signal records: Member ID, Task ID (if applicable), Signal Color (green/red), Count (1-3), Reason, Note (optional), Category, and Creation Date.

06

Zone Classification

Strong, Steady, and Weak zone thresholds

Every team member is classified into a zone based on their Net Score:

Net Score = Effective Strong Signals − Effective Weak Signals
STRONG
Net Score ≥ 3

Strong culture contributor. Consistent delivery and positive signals. This member is thriving and may be a candidate for leadership recognition.

STEADY
Net Score 0 to 2

Neutral or developing. May need coaching or clearer expectations. Not a problem — just an area to watch and support proactively.

WEAK
Net Score < 0

Growth area. This is a coaching cue, not a punishment. Requires a 1-on-1 conversation to understand blockers and provide support.

Important: Zones are about culture health direction, not individual value. A Weak zone means the system has detected patterns that need attention — it's an invitation to help, not to judge.

Task Review Process

Step-by-step guide to reviewing completed tasks

When a task is ready for review, you can assess the quality of the completed work. The review process follows a two-step flow:

Step 1: Select Quality Assessment

Click the review button on a task to open the Review Modal. Choose one option:

Exceptional Contribution +2 Strong Signals

Work exceeded expectations and created outsized impact.

Completed with Excellence +1 Strong Signal

Work met all requirements with solid delivery.

Completed (No Signal) No signals recorded

Just mark as done — no quality assessment needed.

Needs Support +1 Weak Signal

Work needs improvement — a coaching cue.

Blunder +2 Weak Signals

Notable mistakes or poor quality — a stronger coaching cue.

Step 2: Add Category & Note

After selecting the assessment (except "No Signal"), a category dropdown and note field appear:

  • Category — Select a reason category (People, SOP, Emotion, Skill, Domain, Management, Other)
  • Note — Optional context about the assessment

Click Submit Review to record the signal and mark the task as completed.

08

System Mechanics

Signal Recovery, Monthly Attention, and Risk Alerts

Signal Recovery

Rule: 2 consecutive clean months (no weak signals)
Effect: All older weak signals reduced to 50% weight

This rewards sustained improvement and provides a path to a clean slate. Members who demonstrate consistent positive behavior are not permanently penalized by older signals.

Monthly Attention Signal

Rule: 2+ weak signals in a single calendar month
Effect: Attention badge appears on the member's card

This is a pattern indicator, not a punishment. When a member accumulates multiple weak signals in one month, it invites a 1-on-1 coaching conversation to understand what's happening.

Smart Risk Alert

Rule: Assigning a task to a member with 2+ same-category or keyword-matching weak signals this month
Effect: Warning shown on the task form (assignment still allowed)

The system proactively warns you when assigning work to someone who may be struggling in a related area. This helps leaders make informed decisions about workload distribution.

Overdue Auto-Detection

Rule: Task passes its due date without completion
Effect: +1 weak signal automatically recorded

The system checks for overdue tasks and automatically records a weak signal. This ensures accountability without manual intervention. Overdue tasks also appear in the Dashboard's Attention Board.

Reason Categories

Every signal can be tagged with a reason category for pattern analysis. Default categories:

People SOP Emotion Skill Domain Management Other

Categories can be customized in Configuration. They help identify whether issues are systemic (SOP, Management) or individual (Skill, Emotion).

Reports

Executive-level culture intelligence reports

The Reports module provides 6 executive-level analytics views. Use the Period and Department dropdowns to filter data.

1. Monthly Culture Health

Zone distribution, Culture Momentum Index, Risk Index, Stability Score, Net Improvement %, and a list of top/bottom contributors. Shows a sparkline trend of momentum over time.

2. Zone Movement

Transition matrix showing how members moved between zones (Green→Orange, Red→Green, etc.). Includes movement trend bars and notable individual movements.

3. Risk Radar

Members currently at risk (red zone this month), consecutive red zone tracking, department risk density, and category breakdown of weak signals.

4. Leadership Pipeline

Identifies leadership candidates (sustained green zone), high-potential members, and tracks green momentum. Helps with succession planning.

5. Department Health

Department health cards with zone counts, health matrix heatmap across months, and department momentum sparklines. Identifies which departments need attention.

6. Quarterly Intelligence

Executive summary narrative, zone distribution timeline over last 6 months, and quarter-over-quarter comparison table with all key metrics.

Export Options

  • CSV — Downloads a spreadsheet with member data and scores for the selected period
  • PDF — Opens the print dialog for the current report (optimized print layout)
